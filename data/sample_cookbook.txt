Fried Rice

A colorful Nigerian party rice dish with mixed vegetables, chicken, and shrimp.

Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 35 minutes
Servings: 6

Ingredients:
- 3 cups parboiled rice
- 2 large carrots, diced
- 1 cup green beans, chopped
- 1 cup sweet corn
- 1 cup green peas
- 2 medium onions, chopped
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp curry powder
- 1 tsp thyme
- 100ml vegetable oil
- 4 cups chicken stock
- 200g chicken breast, cubed
- 100g shrimp
- 3 seasoning cubes
- Salt to taste

Instructions:
1. Parboil rice until half done, drain and set aside.
2. Heat oil in large pan, add onions and garlic, fry until fragrant.
3. Add chicken pieces, fry until lightly browned.
4. Add carrots, green beans, cook for 3 minutes.
5. Add curry powder, thyme, seasoning cubes, stir well.
6. Add parboiled rice, mix thoroughly.
7. Pour in chicken stock gradually while stirring.
8. Add sweet corn, peas, and shrimp.
9. Cover and simmer on low heat for 20 minutes.
10. Stir occasionally to prevent burning.
11. Serve hot with fried plantains.

Notes:
Use day-old rice for better texture
Don't add too much stock or rice will be mushy
Can add liver or gizzard for extra flavor


Pepper Soup

A spicy, aromatic Nigerian soup perfect for cold evenings or when feeling under the weather.

Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 40 minutes
Servings: 4

Ingredients:
- 1kg goat meat or catfish
- 2 medium onions, sliced
- 4 scotch bonnet peppers
- 2 tbsp ground pepper soup spice
- 1 tbsp ground ehuru (calabash nutmeg)
- 1 tsp ground uziza seeds
- 2 seasoning cubes
- 1 bunch scent leaves, chopped
- 6 cups water
- Salt to taste

Instructions:
1. Wash meat thoroughly and cut into pieces.
2. Place meat in pot with water, onions, and salt.
3. Boil for 30 minutes until tender.
4. Add ground pepper soup spice, ehuru, uziza.
5. Add scotch bonnet peppers (whole or blended).
6. Add seasoning cubes, simmer for 10 minutes.
7. Add chopped scent leaves.
8. Cook for 5 more minutes.
9. Serve hot with white rice or yam.

Notes:
Pepper soup spice is a blend available in African stores
Can use chicken, fish, or assorted meat
The spicier, the better for clearing sinuses


Puff Puff

Sweet, fluffy deep-fried dough balls - Nigeria's favorite snack.

Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Servings: 20 pieces

Ingredients:
- 2 cups flour
- 1/2 cup sugar
- 1 tsp yeast
- 1/4 tsp nutmeg powder
- Pinch of salt
- Warm water (as needed)
- Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:
1. Mix flour, sugar, yeast, nutmeg, and salt in bowl.
2. Add warm water gradually while mixing to form smooth batter.
3. Batter should be thick but pourable.
4. Cover and let rise for 45 minutes to 1 hour.
5. Heat oil in deep pot until very hot.
6. Scoop batter with spoon and drop into hot oil.
7. Fry until golden brown on all sides.
8. Remove and drain on paper towels.
9. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Notes:
Don't make batter too thin or puff puff will absorb oil
Oil must be very hot for best results
Can add vanilla extract for extra flavor
Perfect for parties and gatherings
